A Family Business Background - Understanding Your Struggles

Coming from a Small Business Family

I understand the struggles because I've lived them. Coming from a small business family, I've seen firsthand what business owners really need - more customers, not more confusion.

The Family Business That Started It All

My father's trucking business taught me what small business owners really need. He spent 30 years working with his hands, building something from nothing. He could fix any engine, navigate any route, but when it came to getting customers online, he hit a wall.

He needed customers to find him online. He needed a website that actually worked. He needed to compete with bigger companies that had fancy websites and marketing teams.

He spent thousands of dollars on web developers and marketing "experts" who promised him the world. What he got were websites that looked pretty but brought him zero customers. Phone calls that never came. Money down the drain.

Living Across Small-Town America

I've lived across small-town America and work as a digital transformation expert. Through visiting small businesses in these communities, I've seen the same story everywhere: hardworking owners struggling to bring in customers online.

"I've spent more on this website stuff than I did on my first truck. And I still don't have a single customer who found me online. I don't understand why it's so complicated."

That's when I realized something was fundamentally broken about how small businesses get help online.

The web development industry was built to serve big corporations with unlimited budgets and dedicated IT teams. Small business owners - people who built America with their bare hands - were getting left behind by overcomplicated solutions and inflated prices.

What I Learned From Dad's Struggles

Watching my father's experience taught me three critical things about what small business owners really need:

  • Straight talk, not tech jargon - Dad needed someone to explain things in plain English, not confuse him with technical terms
  • Solutions that actually work - He didn't need a pretty website; he needed a website that brought him customers
  • Fair pricing for real results - He shouldn't have to choose between feeding his family and growing his business
  • Someone who understands small business - The people helping him should know what it's like to make payroll and worry about next month's bills
